SWARM ROBOTICS

Multi-agent robotic systems that collaborate through distributed intelligence

Project Overview

Our swarm robotics project explores the potential of multiple simple robots working together to perform complex tasks through local interactions and emergent collective behavior. Drawing inspiration from natural swarms like ants and bees, we've developed scalable algorithms that enable robust, adaptive, and efficient coordination among dozens of autonomous units.

Key Features

Decentralized Control

The system operates without central control, with each robot making decisions based on local information and simple rules, resulting in resilient, scalable behavior.

Emergent Intelligence

Complex group behaviors emerge from simple interactions between individual robots, enabling the swarm to adapt to changing conditions and solve problems collectively.

Self-healing Capabilities

The swarm maintains functionality even when individual units fail, automatically reorganizing and reassigning tasks to available robots.

REGENERATIVE BRAKING

Energy recovery system that converts kinetic energy into electrical energy

Project Overview

Our regenerative braking project focuses on maximizing energy recovery in electric vehicles and other mobile platforms. By capturing energy typically lost during deceleration and converting it back into usable electricity, we've developed systems that significantly extend range and improve overall energy efficiency of electric mobility solutions.

Key Features

High Efficiency Recovery

Our system achieves up to 70% energy recovery during braking events, significantly outperforming conventional regenerative systems.

Adaptive Control Algorithm

Intelligent software that adjusts recovery rates based on vehicle speed, battery state, and braking intensity for optimal performance and safety.

Universal Integration

Modular design allows for easy integration with various electric vehicle platforms and battery systems with minimal modification.
